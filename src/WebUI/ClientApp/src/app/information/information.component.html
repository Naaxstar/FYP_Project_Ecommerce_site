SKIN TONE

WHAT ARE DIFFERENT SKIN TONE ?
The first step to the dreaded question of 'what color suits my skin tone' is to delete the bookmark you made for an online quiz based on a supposedly sophisticated algorithm. There are three different skin tones.

Second, look at your arm. To help identify the best colors for your skin tone and core wardrobe, you need to determine whether your skin has warm undertones, cool undertones, or neutral undertones. If the veins on your wrist are bluer than green, you have cool skin. If it's more green, your skin is warm-toned. If it's hard to tell, you probably have a neutral skin tone.

It is important to note that skin shade (ie pale skin, olive skin or dark skin) does not necessarily determine your skin tone. Men with dark skin can have cool skin, and men with pale skin can have warm skin. And all the other way around.

HOW DO I KNOW MY SKIN TONE ?
The vein method is the tried-and-true approach most people take to discover their skin tone. Blueish veins indicate cool undertones, whereas green veins mean warm undertones. Neither or both means you likely have neutral skin.

FAST FASHION

What Fast Fashion is ?
Fast fashion is described as low-cost, fashionable apparel that takes inspiration from the runway or celebrity culture and puts it into items in high-street retailers at a rapid pace to fulfil customer demand. The goal is to get the newest models on the market as quickly as possible so that customers may buy them while they're popular and then throw them away after a few wears. It reinforces the notion that wearing the same dress over and over is a fashion faux pas and in order to be current, you must wear the most up-to-date looks at all times. It is an integral element of the poisonous system of overproduction and consumerism that has made fashion one of the most polluting industries on the planet. Let's have a look at the past before we get started on modifying it.

WHY FAST FASHION IS BAD ?
The environmental effect of fast fashion is enormous. Because of the drive to decrease costs and shorten manufacturing times, environmental compromises are more likely to be made. Fast fashion's negative effect includes the use of low-cost, hazardous textile dyes, making the fashion sector, together with agriculture, one of the world's major pollutants of clean water. That's why, throughout the years, Greenpeace has used its detoxifying fashion campaigns to pressure businesses to remove harmful chemicals from their supply chains. Fast fashion's influence is further aided by low-cost fabrics. One of the most common textiles is polyester. It is made from fossil fuels, contributes to global warming, and when washed, can drop microfibres that contribute to the growing amount of plastic in our seas. Even "natural" textiles, however, can be problematic at the scale required by quick fashion. In impoverished areas, conventional cotton requires vast amounts of water and insecticides. Drought concerns arise as a result of this, as well as high stress on water basins and resource rivalry between businesses and local populations.
